The book Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach by Professors James F. Kurose and Keith W. Ross is a comprehensive resource for understanding the complexities of computer networking. This textbook is widely used in both undergraduate and graduate courses and has been translated into 14 languages, making it an invaluable tool for students worldwide.

James F. Kurose is a Distinguished University Professor of Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. He has extensive experience in teaching and research in computer networking and has held numerous visiting scientist positions in the US and abroad.

Keith W. Ross is the Leonard J. Shustek Chair Professor in Computer Science at the Polytechnic Institute of NYU. His research interests include Internet measurement, peer-to-peer systems, social networks, and privacy. He is also the co-founder and original CEO of Wimba, a company developing online multimedia applications for e-learning.
